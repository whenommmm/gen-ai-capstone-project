Gameplay Programming Intern

About us:
We're a small, hand-crafted indie studio (think the spirit of Team Cherry or
Supergiant) building a 2D action-adventure with tight, responsive combat and a
dense interconnected world. The team is ~12 people who care obsessively about
game feel. As an intern you'll sit with the gameplay team and ship real features
that players will actually touch.

About the role:
You'll work on moment-to-moment gameplay: player movement, combat mechanics,
enemy behaviour, and the little details that make controls feel good. You will
prototype quickly, playtest constantly, and iterate with designers and artists.

Must have:
- Solid programming fundamentals in C# (our engine is Unity)
- A portfolio: personal projects, game jam entries, mods, or prototypes we can play
- Genuine understanding of game feel: input handling, hitboxes, timing, juice
- Ability to take a rough design idea and build a playable prototype
- Currently studying computer science, game development, or equivalent self-taught experience

Nice to have:
- Experience with 2D physics, animation systems, or state machines
- Shader work (HLSL / Shader Graph) or VFX
- Familiarity with Godot, Unreal, or a custom engine
- Participation in game jams (Ludum Dare, GMTK, etc.)
- Basic version control with Git
- A love of the genre: 2D action, metroidvania, soulslike, or platformers

Soft skills:
- Thrives in a small, collaborative team where everyone wears many hats
- Takes feedback well and iterates fast without ego
- Communicates clearly with non-programmers (designers, artists)

Domain: Indie game development (2D action-adventure).
